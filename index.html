3D animation’s never just been a job for me. It’s more like a long, ongoing experiment that started over a decade ago here in Colombia. I I began as a 3D Generalist, taking on whatever came my way and figuring things out on the go. That mix of roles ended up being a huge win. It made me flexible, quick to adapt, and solid when it comes to solving weird technical issues or just stepping in wherever needed.

Later on, I dove into the world of 3D Feature Animation, where I got to be part of my first movie. Wild ride, learned a ton, and saw firsthand how big stories come to life.

Since then, it’s been a mix of cool projects shipped a game, worked on a feature, did some TV shows, and a bunch of commercials. Went from Animator to Senior, then Lead Animator, where I got to help shape full sequences and keep the team energy strong. Lately, I’ve been deep into Unreal Engine, trying to mix tech with that bold, punchy style that makes animation really land.

When I’m not animating someone throwing a punch or freaking out, I’m probably out cycling to clear my head. And if movies come up, just a heads-up: I’ll probably go on about Interstellar. I’m always up for a fun creative challenge or just sharing ideas with people who love this stuff too.
